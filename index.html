Quickstart: Protect your University account with 2FA

Register for 2FA

Make sure you have your smartphone or tablet with you as you follow the next steps. The following steps must be completed on a device other than you smartphone or tablet.

  1. Download the Google Authenticator from the Google Play Store or the App Store.

  2. DO NOT OPEN THE AUTHENTICATOR AT THIS TIME

  3. Go to My UD Settings and select Change my two-factor authentication settings.

  4. Under Method for receiving authentication code set the Google Authenticator app as the Primary method.

  5. Select a Backup method that you have access to.

  6. Look for the Google Authenticator app section on the same page. Select Scan a QR code for the configuration method.

  7. Open your Google Authenticator on you personal device and select Initial setup.

  8. Select the option to Scan a QR code. Scan the QR code that is displayed on the screen for your account.

  9. Go to the Scratch codes section. Print the 10 one-time use 2FA codes to use in an emergency or if you are traveling internationally.

  10. At the bottom of the page, click Finish & submit.

Other tips

  • 2FA codes are time-dependent. When selecting backup communication methods, choose ones that you or a trusted person can access within five minutes.

  • Scratch codes are for emergency use when you cannot access 2FA codes through other means. Scratch codes will not expire, but each code can be used only one time. Using codes from top to bottom and striking through used codes will avoid causing confusion.

  • Changing your 2FA settings will generate a notice sent to your UD email address.

  • Not sure if you are enrolled in 2FA? Log in to My UD Settings. Your 2FA status is listed under My account information.

  • 2FA is required for certain UD services for faculty and employees and for Student Financial forms. It will be required for all students by Jan. 3, 2018.

If you require further assistance with the instructions or information on this page, contact the IT Support Center Link opens in a new window.
